Yes. Each instrument has been published in peer-reviewed journals with established reliability (Cronbach's alpha) and validity. The IPIP-50 and IPIP-NEO-120 are based on Goldberg (1992) and Johnson (2014), the GSE on Schwarzer & Jerusalem (1995), the Grit-S on Duckworth & Quinn (2009), and the UWES-9 on Schaufeli, Bakker & Salanova (2006).

No. All computation happens entirely in your browser using JavaScript. No data is transmitted to any server, no cookies are set, and nothing is stored. When you close the page, your responses are gone. You can save your results by taking a screenshot or printing the page.
The IPIP items are fully public domain ‚Äî free for any use without licensing fees. The GSE, Grit-S, and UWES-9 are freely available for research and non-commercial use, but may have licensing nuances for commercial deployment. Always check the original authors' terms if using commercially.
Percentiles are computed using published population means and standard deviations, transformed via Z-score and normal CDF approximation.
